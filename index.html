About me

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Earth and Planetary Sciences at Harvard University. I did field work as an undergraduate at Michigan Technological University in the beautiful Upper Peninsula of Michigan. Now I work primarily with computer models in the Atmospheric Chemistry Modeling Group at Harvard.

My research areas include atmospheric chemistry, climate policy, and data science. I work with geospatial data and satellite observations to investigate methane emission sources including livestock, oil and gas, coal mining, and landfills.
